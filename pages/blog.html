Hazel Hummingbird: La Colibrí Hazel header

by Nattalie Rochester

The first book in the Mango Tales series is Hazel Hummingbird. It is a garden adventure for the whole family. Hazel starts her day in the waking garden, quickly flying here and there, making stops at her favourite flowers, bugs and bees, while children quietly gaze into the wonderful garden world. At the end of a good day, the children revel in the sunset and say goodbye so they can all settle down to rest. Pre-readers and Early Readers will enjoy:

  • The Hummingbird
  • Tropical Animals and Flowers
  • Directions
  • Colours

Hazel Hummingbird: La Colibrí Hazel , is written in English and Spanish, and vividly illustrated by Coretta Singer,a Swiss-born, Jamaican-grown, self-taught 3D animator, digital artist; and Jerome Marshall, Jamaican-born and raised graphic designer, and Fine Arts graduate of Edna Manley College of the Visual and Performing Arts .

Jamaican-born author Natallie Rochester was inspired to write Hazel Hummingbird: La Colibrí Hazel when she found few books for her daughter with Caribbean scenes and people. Through the picture book and related songs written and produced in Jamaica, she shares the beauty of Caribbean people, sights and sounds with the world. The story gives the world a taste of Natallie’s experience growing up in Jamaica. She grew up fascinated by the Caribbean’s natural sights and sounds. These memories stayed with her even when she was away from home. The passion to share this Caribbean legacy and heritage has inspired the creation of Hazel Hummingbird: La Colibrí Hazel and the vision to increase diverse books, music and other media for children.

Creating the Music We all learn in different ways, and music is a universal language. Here at Mango Tales, we wanted to create a unique, multisensory experience. Over months of inspiration and working with Caribbean artists and producers, we came up with a selection of songs which take us deeper into the special moments of Hazel’s Day. So, we invite you to dance to Caribbean rhythms and fun lyrics with music that brings our story to life. The English text was converted to Braille using regular grade 2 English Braille, American edition. Audible image text descriptions were used to describe the pictures and the scenery to assist the reader in creating a mental picture. In order to create tactile images of the pictures in the Hazel Hummingbird book, the early sketches of selected pages were extracted and the material cleaned to create distinction in the shapes. The selected images of the Braille book were then produced on swell touch paper passed through a heater. To create the raised effect, the images were darkened to attract heat and swell.
